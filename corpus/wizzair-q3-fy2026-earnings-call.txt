Earnings call transcript: Wizz Air Q3 2026 shows net loss improvement
Wizz Air Holdings PLC reported its third-quarter fiscal 2026 results, revealing a net loss of 139 million euros, a 42% improvement year-over-year. Revenue grew 10% to 1.3 billion euros. The company’s stock is currently trading at $19.69, up from its previous close of $19.41, and sits 45% above its 52-week low of $13.26. According to InvestingPro analysis, Wizz Air appears fairly valued based on its current Fair Value assessment.

Key Takeaways
Wizz Air reported a significant improvement in net loss year-over-year.
Revenue increased by 10%, reaching 1.3 billion euros.
The company closed bases in Abu Dhabi and Vienna, shifting focus to Central and Eastern Europe.
Wizz Air plans to transition to a 100% A321 fleet by 2025.
Company Performance
Wizz Air’s third-quarter performance showed notable improvements despite posting a net loss. The airline’s revenue increased by 10%, driven by a 12% rise in passenger numbers and an 11.1% increase in capacity. The company strengthened its cash position to 2 billion euros and repaid a 500 million euro outstanding bond. Wizz Air continues to focus on expanding its presence in Central and Eastern Europe, closing bases in Abu Dhabi and Vienna, and opening new ones in Bratislava, Podgorica, Yerevan, and Warsaw.

Financial Highlights
Revenue: 1.3 billion euros, up 10% year-over-year
Net Loss: 139 million euros, a 42% improvement year-over-year
EBITDA: Increased by 5%, maintaining a margin of 13.6%
Passenger Numbers: Up 12%
Capacity (ASK): Increased by 11.1%
Outlook & Guidance
Looking ahead to fiscal 2027, Wizz Air expects a capacity increase of around 10%, with the load factor and RASK remaining flat. The airline anticipates a net profit in the breakeven range, between a 25 million euro loss and a 25 million euro profit. The company is also strategizing a re-entry into the Ukraine market, targeting 15 million seat capacity by Year 3.
Executive Commentary
CEO József Váradi highlighted the fleet transition, stating, "We are two years away from uplifting the entire aircraft." He also noted the company’s strategic focus, saying, "We are not in the compensation business." CFO Ian Malin emphasized operational efficiency, remarking, "Reliability is a RASK lever."

Risks and Challenges
Fleet Transition: The shift to a 100% A321 fleet by 2025 poses logistical and financial challenges.
Market Re-entry: The strategy for re-entering the Ukraine market involves significant risk and uncertainty.
Competitive Pressure: Despite minimal competitive pressure currently, market dynamics may change.
Maintenance Costs: Rising maintenance costs could impact financial performance.
Currency Fluctuations: Currency hedging strategies will be crucial in managing financial risk.
Overall, Wizz Air’s third-quarter results reflect a positive trajectory in revenue growth and operational efficiency, despite ongoing challenges in the airline industry.
