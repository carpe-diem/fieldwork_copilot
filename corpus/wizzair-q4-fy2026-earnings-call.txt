Earnings call transcript: Wizz Air Q4 2026 sees stock dip amid mixed signals
Wizz Air Holdings PLC (WIZZ) reported its Q4 2026 financial results, revealing robust cash generation and improved leverage ratios. Despite these positive indicators, the company’s stock fell by 2.46%, reflecting investor concerns over increased maintenance costs and future earnings forecasts. The stock is trading near its 52-week low of $11.14, highlighting cautious market sentiment. InvestingPro data reveals the stock has declined 24% year-to-date and 21% over the past year, with shares currently 67% below their 52-week high of $19.45.

Key Takeaways
Wizz Air’s cash generation reached EUR 1 billion, a 22% increase year-over-year.
The leverage ratio improved from 4.4x to 3.7x net debt to EBITDA.
Despite revenue growth, maintenance and depreciation costs increased.
The stock dropped 2.46%, trading near its 52-week low.
Company Performance
Wizz Air demonstrated solid performance in Q4 2026, with a notable increase in cash generation and an improved balance sheet. The company carried 70 million passengers, marking a 10% year-over-year increase. Revenue grew by 8%, supported by a 10.5% increase in seat capacity. However, maintenance costs and depreciation rose, impacting overall profitability.

Financial Highlights
Revenue: Increased by 8% year-over-year.
Pre-tax profit: EUR 27 million, showing positive improvement.
EBITDA: Up 16% year-over-year.
Free cash flow: EUR 1 billion, a 22% increase year-over-year.
Net cash position: Over EUR 2 billion.
Market Reaction
Wizz Air’s stock fell by 2.46%, with a decrease of 106 points from the last close value. The stock is trading close to its 52-week low, reflecting investor concerns over future earnings forecasts and increased maintenance costs. The market reaction suggests cautious sentiment despite the company’s strong cash generation and improved leverage ratio. Outlook & Guidance
Looking ahead, Wizz Air anticipates challenges with future earnings forecasts showing potential negative EPS of -$0.39 for FY2026, despite trailing twelve-month EPS of $3.71. The company plans to continue its fleet modernization, aiming for over 90% NEO aircraft by the end of FY2027. The strategic partnership with SpaceX for Starlink Managed Service is expected to enhance ancillary revenue.

Executive Commentary
CEO József Váradi stated, "Our strong cash generation and improved leverage ratio demonstrate the resilience of our business model. We remain focused on optimizing our fleet and enhancing passenger experience through innovative partnerships."

Risks and Challenges
Increased maintenance costs due to prior accrual reversals.
Higher depreciation costs from A320ceo retirements.
Potential negative EPS forecasts for future quarters.
Market uncertainties impacting capacity and growth strategies.
Ongoing resolution of GTF engine issues affecting aircraft availability.
Q&A
During the earnings call, analysts questioned the impact of maintenance cost increases and the strategic rationale behind the Starlink partnership. Management emphasized the long-term benefits of fleet modernization and ancillary revenue opportunities from the SpaceX collaboration.
